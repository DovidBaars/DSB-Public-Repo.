About Me

I am entering the Israel Tech Challenge frontend bootcamp because I want to integrate myself into the hi-tech workforce, and put myself on a sustainable path to be proficient in the tools needed to develop online programs, and content delivery systems using modern, and sophisticated methods.

In the past ten years I have been a Jewish Educator at The University Of Illinois Champaign Urbana, a mentor and student liaison for an out-of-the-box yeshiva in the Nachlaot neighborhood of Jerusalem, co-directed a school developing the next generation of Jewish world leaders located in Israel, China, and India, and facilitated dozens of men towards health through experiential workshops, and private sessions.

I love the ways music can bring us towards embodiment. I play hand drums, the ukulele, harmonica, and egg shakers. I ferment home-made wine, kombucha, sourdough, and all kinds of pickles. I practice leading people into deeper relationships with themselves through music, poetry, meditation, and ceremony.

I am blessed to be married to my soul-partner Leah, and with our three perfect children, Chana Bracha, Yosef Shalom, and Adina.
